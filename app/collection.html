Weaving a realm


Vietnam Centre’s symbol is a stylized Thái Bình Kết/ Phương Thằng - a string with no start end points. This is an ancient Orient symbol, treasured by Vietnamese people of the last centuries. Ancient Vietnamese like decorating their personal belongings and interior furniture with a lot of “Thai binh ket” with hopes for never ending peace and wealth.

Vietnam Centre wants Vietnamese culture to live on like the string with no end.





vietnam center & đại việt cổ phong

February, 2018

Talking about Vietnamese traditional costume, it must be “ao dai”, “ao tu than”... Actually, our traditional costumes are not that simple. Along several thousand years of history and social and cultural change, Vietnamese costumes also features different characteristics in each period. “Weaving a realm” is a project to perform rituals and imperial costumes of Dai Viet dynasty in the beginning of Le So period, from 1437 to 1471. It is a 2 hour show. Vietnam Centre has remodeled these costumes based on credited research.






The project aims to introduce to international friends and also Vietnamese overseas the beauty of Vietnam’s top culture in a period transitioning from a colony to an influential nation in the region. This show will take place in February 2018 in Sydney with future plans to extend to other countries around the world.








Ma Chau Silk

Not far from Hoi iAn, World Cultural Heritage, Ma Chau (Quang Nam province) is one of the biggest silk production centers in Vietnam. It is not a coincidence that these two locations are so near. Hoi An and Ma Chau are key to the prosperity of one of the busiest trading areas in Pacific Asia in the 17th century. They are also cornerstones for the last dynasty of Vietnam.
